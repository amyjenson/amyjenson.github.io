Amy Jenson

I recently graduated with a M.S. in mathematics from the Department of Mathematical Sciences at Montana State University. I worked with Dr. Scott McCalla and Dr. Mark Skidmore on a M.S. thesis modeling brine flow through ice-walled channels.

My research interests lie in subglacial hydrology and modeling different aspects of drainage such as outburst floods and channel evolution as well as how subglacial hydrology influences ice dynamics.

In the fall of 2022, I will join the Glaciers Group at the Geophysical Institute and University of Alaska Fairbanks to pursue a PhD in Geophysics with Dr. Martin Truffer .
