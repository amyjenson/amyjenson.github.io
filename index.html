Amy Jenson

In the fall of 2022, I joined the Glaciers Group at the Geophysical Institute and University of Alaska Fairbanks to pursue a PhD in Geophysics with Dr. Martin Truffer. I am exploring the role of subglacial hydrology on seasonal velocity variability on tidewater glaciers using coupled ice-flow and subglacial hydrology models in Elmer/Ice.

I graduated with a M.S. in mathematics from the Department of Mathematical Sciences at Montana State University in Spring 2022. I worked with Dr. Scott McCalla and Dr. Mark Skidmore on a M.S. thesis modeling brine flow through ice-walled channels. I received a Bachelors in Science in Mathematics from University of Alaska Southeast in Spring 2020.

My research interests lie in subglacial hydrology and modeling different aspects of drainage such as outburst floods and channel evolution as well as how subglacial hydrology influences ice dynamics.
